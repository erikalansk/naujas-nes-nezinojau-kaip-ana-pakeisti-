Addiction to social media and how to control it

Photograph by Erika Lanskaronskyte

There are a lot of addictions these days. Addiction to drugs, alcohol, coffee, tobacco and even to social media platforms. It may seem that the last addiction cannot be compared to the other listed substance use disorders, but this is false. Research shows that over 70% of my interviewed people are addicted to social media sites. Although in the previous article “Impact of social media on mental health” we talked about addiction to SoMe, this time we will dive deeper and find out how this addiction occurs and how it should be controlled.

Addiction to social media, what is it?

Social media has the same impact on people as gambling, recreational drugs etc. It makes consumers use the product or service as much as possible. Studies have shown that the constant stream of retweets, likes, and shares from SoMe sites have affected the brain’s reward area to trigger the same kind of chemical reaction as other drugs, such as cocaine. When an individual gets a notification, such as a like or mention, it makes him or her feel pleasure. The more pleasure you feel from likes, comments, etc. the more addicted you get

Social media usage becomes a problem, when a person looks at it as a way to cope with stress, loneliness or even anxiety and depression. When you feel better using SoMe than you feel in your real life, you end up engaging in activity more and more. And this kind of activity usually leads to multiple personal mental and physical health problems, it affects our real-life relationships, our performance in school or work.

“If you feel the need to constantly post on social media documenting your every move, chances are you're either addicted to social networking or there is a void somewhere in your personal life.” ― Germany Kent

Controlling the addiction

Now when you read how this addiction occurs you may want to know how to control it. It is important to know that this substance use disorder is very much treatable. So, there is no need to give up in any way. Here are some ways to detach yourself from social media:


  1. Turn off your notifications – it will be easier to concentrate on your daily tasks.
  2. Limit yourself – set a timer and limit the amount of time you spend on social media.
  3. Check in with friends and family – meet them instead of looking at their pictures or chatting with them using social media platforms.
  4. Get a new hobby - you will have less time for social media, and you will stop mindlessly scrolling through your newsfeed.
  5. Delete apps you don’t need – it will remove temptation to scroll.
  6. Make it a treat – use social media only when you accomplished something.
  7. Go on holiday – you can go on literal vacation without a phone, but you can also pick a period of time you are not going to use social media and then delete all the apps.

Hopefully now you feel more educated on addiction to social media. And if you think you are addicted to social platforms try using the tips given above and if it doesn’t help, contact a dedicated treatment provider.
